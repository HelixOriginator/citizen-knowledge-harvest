00 · Humanity's Blind Spot

We track everything. Except how we learn

Every modern institution exists to measure something. Money. Output. Population. Emissions. Yet the activity that makes all of those things improve over time — human learning — has no ledger, no registry, no infrastructure of its own.

We track money

Central banks, ledgers, audits, GDP

We track products

Supply chains, barcodes, patents

We track populations

Census, registries, demographics

We track emissions

Sensors, satellites, climate models

LEDGER 1 · EXISTS

The Financial Ledger

  • Every transaction recorded
  • Standardized units of account
  • Audited, regulated, trusted
  • Built over 600 years, since double-entry bookkeeping
  • Powers credit, trade, taxation, policy
LEDGER 2 · MISSING

The Knowledge Ledger

  • No transaction is recorded
  • No standardized unit of insight
  • Unaudited, unregulated, untrusted
  • Never systematically attempted, until now
  • Could power education, policy, and innovation

This is civilization's largest unaddressed information gap. CKH exists to close it.

02 · Invisible Research Theory

Two systems of human knowledge

Knowledge exists in parallel. One is celebrated, archived, and rewarded. The other is lived, lost, and forgotten. CKH bridges both.

Definition

A theory describing how legitimate, useful knowledge is continuously produced outside credentialed institutions, through lived practice rather than formal study.

Metric

Invisible-to-Visible Ratio — the estimated 1,300:1 gap between daily undocumented experiments and annual peer-reviewed papers.

Real Example

A mechanic's ear for engine sound predicts failure days before any diagnostic tool — knowledge that exists nowhere but in practice.

Civilizational Impact

Closing this gap could surface entire fields of applied science that institutions have never had access to.

03 · The Knowledge Asset Class

The largest unrecognized asset in the world

Economists measure three forms of capital. There is a fourth — larger than the other three combined — that has never been counted.

Physical Capital

Machines, buildings, infrastructure. Measured for centuries. Fully on the ledger.

Financial Capital

Cash, credit, equity. Tracked to the cent, globally, in real time.

Human Capital

Education, skills, credentials. Partially tracked through degrees and resumes.

Knowledge Capital

Tacit expertise, adaptation, problem-solving. Almost entirely untracked.

Definition

Knowledge capital is the stock of practical, undocumented intelligence held by individuals and communities — distinct from credentials, which only certify a fraction of it.

Metric

Knowledge GDP — the estimated economic value generated by knowledge that never entered a formal market or ledger.

Real Example

An informal trader's pricing intuition, built over a decade, outperforms textbook models — yet appears on no balance sheet anywhere.

Civilizational Impact

Recognizing knowledge capital could redirect investment, policy, and education toward where real expertise already lives.

10 · Knowledge Extinction Tracker · The Extinction of Experience

Knowledge dies the same way species do

When the last practitioner of a technique passes without passing it on, that knowledge is gone — permanently, the way a species is gone. CKH tracks this loss with the same urgency conservation tracks biodiversity.

Definition

A live risk register for knowledge systems whose last carriers are aging out faster than the knowledge is being documented.

Metric

Knowledge Risk Score (0–100), combining carrier age, documentation rate, and successor count into an estimated Time to Loss.
